Home insurance sample

Home insurance may cover buildings, contents, or both. Covered events often include fire, storm, theft, escape of water, and accidental damage if included in the policy.

After water damage, the policyholder should stop the water source if safe, protect undamaged property, take photos, keep damaged items for inspection, and notify the insurer promptly.

A deductible is the amount the policyholder pays before the insurer contributes to a covered claim. Depreciation, deductibles, and policy limits can all reduce the final payout.

Mold may be excluded if it results from gradual damage, poor maintenance, or delayed repairs. It may be considered when directly linked to a covered sudden event, depending on policy wording.
