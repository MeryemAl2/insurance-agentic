Insurance FAQ sample

An exclusion is policy wording that removes cover for specific events, causes, property, people, or uses.

Third-party motor insurance generally covers legal liability to others for injury or property damage. It usually does not pay for damage to the policyholder's own vehicle.

Policy cancellation rules depend on the contract and local regulation. A customer may need to give written notice, and refunds may be prorated or reduced by fees.

The claim form usually asks for policy details, claimant details, date and location of loss, description of what happened, estimated amount, supporting documents, and signatures.
