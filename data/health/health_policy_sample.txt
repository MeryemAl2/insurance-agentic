Health insurance sample

Health insurance may cover eligible hospitalization, emergency treatment, surgery, diagnostic tests, and prescribed medicines according to the plan benefits.

Many health policies contain waiting periods. A waiting period is a time after policy start when selected benefits are not yet payable.

Pre-existing condition clauses can restrict cover for illnesses, injuries, or symptoms that existed before the policy start date. The exact definition depends on the policy wording.

Some non-emergency treatments require pre-authorization. If pre-authorization is missing, the policyholder should ask the insurer for the reason for denial, collect medical records, invoices, and referral letters, and submit an appeal within the policy deadline.
